Meet Michael Maraia

A life turned into music.

Michael Maraia is a United States Army veteran, singer, and songwriter behind MyZ Backroads. His music is rooted in lived experience—survival, recovery, faith, loneliness, courage, and the choice to keep going.

Every song begins with a real feeling or a real chapter. Some are heavy. Some are hopeful. All of them are honest.

“The backroads are where the noise fades, the truth gets louder, and the next chapter begins.”

Backroads of My Life

A life story told one mile marker at a time.

A respectful timeline of the places, people, service, losses, healing, and country music that shaped MyZ Backroads.

Age 12

Began singing after surviving

After surviving a suicide attempt, singing became part of recovery, survival, and learning how to keep going.

Florida children’s home

The day country music found me

A new friend introduced Michael to country music, opening a doorway to stories that felt honest and close to home.

Early 1980s

First country concert

Kenny Rogers and Tammy Wynette showed how a song could carry tenderness, grit, and a whole life in three minutes.

Soon after

Dolly’s country-field stage

Seeing Dolly Parton in concert left a lasting image: a beautiful country-field stage where music felt like home.

Younger adulthood

Finding identity through the voice

Michael kept singing, growing, and shaping an identity rooted in honesty, faith, and country storytelling.

Weaverville, North Carolina

Eighteen years in the mountains

For approximately 18 years, Weaverville was home: a place of daily life, work, writing, memory, and music.

Home studio years

Where stories became songs

A personal recording and creative space turned limited resources into demos, ideas, and finished country stories.

Army service

Service, sacrifice, and memory

Life in and after service shaped the writing with themes of sacrifice, resilience, brotherhood, and what follows a soldier home.

Horse therapy

Peace, trust, and healing

Horses offered quiet lessons in patience, trust, presence, and peace when words were not enough.

Where the Music Grew

Small room. Big stories.

Long before MyZ Backroads had a website, the music grew inside a small home studio in Weaverville, North Carolina. It was crowded, personal, imperfect, and full of ideas. This is where stories became songs.

Singing at the microphone, building tracks with limited resources, and writing from lived experience became the foundation. Even after Hurricane Helene took much of Michael’s history and belongings, the songs kept coming.
