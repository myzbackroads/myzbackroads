My Country Story

A life turned into music.

Michael Maraia is a United States Army veteran, singer, and songwriter behind MyZ Backroads. His music is rooted in lived experience—survival, recovery, faith, loneliness, courage, and the choice to keep going.

Every song begins with a real feeling or a real chapter. Some are heavy. Some are hopeful. All of them are honest.

Backroads of My Life: the long roads, quiet turns, and hard-earned lessons behind these songs shape the sound of MyZ Backroads.

Horse Therapy: horses, trust, and stillness remain part of the healing story woven through this music.

“The backroads are where the noise fades, the truth gets louder, and the next chapter begins.”

Behind the Songs

Stories that can keep growing.

Each expandable note is ready for longer memories, photos, or future video links without redesigning the page.

Embrace Yourself

Inspired by the moment a person decides their scars, history, and heart are not things to hide—they are proof they are still here.

Combat Cowboy

Written from the veteran road, where service does not end when the uniform comes off and courage becomes part of daily life.

Let It Go

Built around the hard freedom of forgiving what cannot be changed and choosing not to carry every mile behind you.

A Friend I Used to Know

A remembrance of trust, distance, loss, and the way certain people remain part of your song even after life changes.

Along the Way

A recovery song about consequences, survival, and the lessons that only show up after the darkest roads.

The Lord Kept Me

A country-gospel testimony for anyone who looks back and knows they did not make it through alone.

Hug a Horse

A tribute to the quiet therapy of horses: patience, trust, breath, and the kind of peace words cannot always reach.

Men Like Me

A song about being honest enough to be seen and brave enough to belong without pretending.

Leave While You Can

A protective story song for people who need permission to trust what they know and choose safety.
